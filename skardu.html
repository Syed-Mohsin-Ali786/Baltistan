Skardu

The name Skardu is believed to be derived from a Balti word meaning "a lowland between two high places." The two referenced high places are Shigar city and the high-altitude Satpara Lake.

Locally, the name may be written as སྐར་དོ་ based on pronunciation. Its meaning corresponds to the Tibetan word མདོ. Over time, in the evolution of the Balti language, the prefix sound /m/ was lost and the vowel /o/ shifted to /u/, similar to changes seen in other Tibetan dialects.
